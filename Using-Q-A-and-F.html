Using Question, Answer, and Feedback

Module Goal & Objectives

Goal: This module will help you move beyond simple information presentation to using questions, answers, and feedback to ensure genuine learning is happening.

Objectives: By the end of this module, you'll be able to:

  • Use question, answer, and feedback techniques effectively.
  • Discuss the two-way communication flow between student and instructor.
  • Describe the benefits of preparing written questions.
  • Identify the best times for student questions and when to postpone them.
  • Develop questions that avoid simple 'yes' or 'no' answers.
  • List reasons for instructors to ask questions and provide appropriate feedback.
  • Describe eight distinct types of questions.

This section introduces the foundational elements of effective teaching.

ü§î What are Questions, Answers, and Feedback?

Question: A request for information.

Answer: A spoken, written, or non-verbal response to a question.

Feedback: A reaction to a process or activity. It's a two-way street between instructors and students!

üöÄ How to Adopt Effective Habits Early On

  • Welcome inquiry: Start every class by asking for questions.
  • Be prepared: Write down questions for each major topic.
  • Encourage effort: Design questions that require critical thinking, not just a "yes" or "no."

Learn how to manage student-initiated questions to maximize learning.

‚è±Ô∏è When should students ask questions?

Encourage students to ask questions as soon as they arise, especially if they are relevant to the current topic. If needed, you can coach the class to hold questions for specific times.

ü§∑ How should you handle student questions?

  • Redirect: If time allows, redirect the question to the class to start a discussion.
  • Answer directly: If time is short, answer it yourself and move on.
  • Postpone (if necessary): It's okay to say, "We'll cover that later," but only if you genuinely will.
  • Observe feedback: Watch the student's cues to ensure your answer was understood.

Explore why and how you, as the instructor, should be asking questions.

üéØ How should you manage student answers?

For correct answers: Affirm the response and briefly explain why it's correct to reinforce the learning.

For partially correct answers: Acknowledge the correct parts first, then provide the complete, correct answer. You can also redirect to the class to build the full answer together.

This section details eight distinct types of questions to achieve different learning goals.

Question Type Description
Ask - Pause - Call Ask a question, pause to scan the class, then call on a student.
Bridge Creates a logical connection between topics.
Closed A question with very few possible answers.
Direct A question aimed at a specific student.
Open A question with many possible answers to encourage broad thinking.
Overhead A question posed to the entire class.
Relay Redirecting a student's question to the rest of the class.
Rhetorical A question asked to stimulate thought without needing a verbal answer.

Conclusion

Effective teaching thrives on the exchange of information. Make it a regular practice to ask questions, and create an environment where students feel comfortable doing the same. Remember, feedback is constant and flows in both directions!
